K‚Äì2 Global Tech-Enhanced Collaboration Activities

‚ñº

Communication & Listening

  • Record short Flip videos responding to a partner class's "Question of the Week."
  • Use picture-supported Padlet walls to react to classmates' and partner classes' posts with emojis or simple sentences.
  • Practice paraphrasing by using voice typing to repeat what a partner said and checking it together.

Roles & Participation

  • Use color-coded digital whiteboards (Jamboard alternatives, FigJam) where each student adds one item in turn.
  • Assign simple digital roles like "Sticker Adder," "Idea Recorder," or "Photo Taker" during joint activities.

People Skills (Perspective-Taking, Repair, Empathy)

  • Students send simple "kind replies" on Seesaw to a partner class's artwork or stories.
  • Use AI to generate a friendly "How might someone else feel?" prompt after students describe a disagreement.

Project Management

  • Create simple shared checklists (Google Keep with images) for joint projects like plant growth observations.
  • Use a classroom timer to help groups take turns adding photos or labels to a shared digital poster.

Problem-Solving & Reasoning

  • Use digital story sequencing tools with a partner class, each group placing a card and explaining why.
  • Ask AI: "Give us 2 different ways kids in another place might solve this puzzle."

Reflection & Metacognition

  • Record 10-second reflection clips on Flip to share what their group did well.
  • Use QR-linked reflection forms with picture choices for how teamwork felt.

Tools & Technology

  • Create a shared digital museum of class moments using teacher-managed Padlet, with contributions from another K‚Äì2 class.
  • Practice replaying directions via QR codes and teaching the step to another grade level.

Grades 3‚Äì5 Global Tech-Enhanced Collaboration Activities

‚ñº

Communication & Listening

  • Participate in cross-class Flip discussions where students must respond with a paraphrase and one clarifying question.
  • Use AI to translate simple messages between classes in different languages and compare phrasing to check understanding.

Roles & Participation

  • Assign digital roles (Researcher, Recorder, Slide Designer) with rotation tracked in a shared Google Doc.
  • Use FigJam or Miro boards where each student contributes one sticky note per round to ensure balanced airtime.

People Skills (Perspective-Taking, Repair, Empathy)

  • Exchange "community snapshots" with another school via Padlet and ask empathy-oriented questions about similarities and differences.
  • Use a chat simulation to practice conflict repair phrases like "Let's reset" or "Go ahead ‚Äî I think I interrupted."

Project Management

  • Use a joint Trello board with another class where each site manages its own column but reviews the other's progress.
  • Share timelines in Google Sheets and hold asynchronous check-ins through comments.

Problem-Solving & Reasoning

  • Exchange local data (weather, plant growth, school lunch waste) with another school and compare patterns using Sheets or Excel.
  • Use AI to generate "What if this problem happened in a different community?" prompts.

Reflection & Metacognition

  • Build a co-authored Book Creator reflection journal with a partner class.
  • Use Flip to record progress updates and tag them with "what we improved" labels.

Tools & Technology

  • Create a joint Google Site where both classes curate resources or document a project.
  • Students create video directions for a math or science activity and send them to another classroom to test.

Grades 6‚Äì8 Global Tech-Enhanced Collaboration Activities

‚ñº

Communication & Listening

  • Hold asynchronous debates with another school using Padlet, Flip, or Teams; students must paraphrase the argument before responding.
  • Use AI to check tone and clarity before posting to a shared collaboration space.

Roles & Participation

  • Split cross-school teams into functional roles (design, research, communication) and manage roles with Notion or Trello.
  • Conduct "whiteboard relays" where groups in two schools take turns adding layers to a FigJam design.

People Skills (Perspective-Taking, Repair, Empathy)

  • Partner with another school to exchange "community challenges" and brainstorm empathetic solutions.
  • Use AI for perspective-swap practice: "Explain the issue from the viewpoint of the other group."

Project Management

  • Conduct a multi-school design sprint with shared Kanban boards and time-boxed tasks.
  • Share draft artifacts via Google Drive and use comment-only mode for structured peer feedback.

Problem-Solving & Reasoning

  • Collaborate on an international data comparison project (air quality, biodiversity, noise levels) via shared spreadsheets.
  • Use Scratch or Tinkercad to prototype a joint invention, with each class owning alternating development phases.

Reflection & Metacognition

  • Students contribute to a shared Notion or Book Creator "Collaboration Log" highlighting process insights.
  • AI-coded reflection summaries combine responses from both schools to identify themes.

Tools & Technology

  • Maintain a joint digital portfolio of project work across classrooms and revise based on shared feedback.
  • Use QR directions created by one class and tested by another to evaluate clarity.

Grades 9‚Äì12 Global Tech-Enhanced Collaboration Activities

‚ñº

Communication & Listening

  • Participate in global discussion forums with partner schools where students respond to complex prompts using evidence and paraphrase.
  • Use AI tools to analyze communication logs for patterns of collaboration, tone, or misunderstanding.

Roles & Participation

  • Form cross-school project teams using shared workspace platforms (Teams, Slack EDU, Notion) with clearly defined responsibilities.
  • Students lead virtual meetings using collaborative agendas and live note-taking tools.

People Skills (Perspective-Taking, Repair, Empathy)

  • Conduct digital cultural interviews with international peers and use structured protocols to interpret multiple viewpoints.
  • Practice conflict repair by role-playing real miscommunication scenarios in chat tools.

Project Management

  • Run multi-week design or research projects with Trello or Monday.com EDU, coordinating timelines across schools or community partners.
  • Use shared Gantt charts to synchronize milestones and distribute responsibilities.

Problem-Solving & Reasoning

  • Collaborate on solving authentic community issues (transportation, environmental monitoring) using shared datasets and AI modeling.
  • Co-design digital prototypes (Figma, Canva, Tinkercad) with students from another region or country.

Reflection & Metacognition

  • Build a combined digital publication (Google Sites, Sway) that documents the collaboration process across all partner schools.
  • Use AI-coded analysis of reflections from multiple classrooms to identify recurring insights and challenges.

Tools & Technology

  • Produce a joint digital showcase for community or global audiences, using multimedia contributions from each school.
  • Develop clear, accessible digital instruction sets for another school to pilot and critique.
